Aruba: One Happy Island

One of many islands in the caribbean sea, Aruba is a place of serenity. From the bright pink flamingos to the crystal blue waters, this island is a must visit.

History

In 1636, it was taken by the Dutch and occupied by the Dutch West India Company. As part of the Netherlands Antilles, Aruba came briefly under British rule during the Napoleonic Wars but was returned to the Netherlands in 1816.

Cuisine

  • Pastechi - Known as Aruba's national snack, they are a take on the latin empanada
  • Fresh Seafood - Fishermen haul in wahoo, snapper, shrimp, lobster, oysters, mahi-mahi, grouper, conch, and more fresh every day
  • Dutch Pancakes- Similar to crepes, locals eat pannenkoeken filled with apple, bananas, or pineapples, for breakfast. For lunch or dinner, islanders dine on heartier versions with salami, ham, cheese, or tuna.
  • Pica di Papaya - Some like it hot. Very hot. If you do, try pica di papaya, an Aruban hot sauce. Key ingredients are the locally farmed Madame Jeanette peppers and papayas. The fruit adds a sweet note to the heat.

        4. Palm Island

        Palm Island is off the coast of Aruba's shore. It's beautiful sandy beach has made it rise in popularity as of recent years. It has become a tourist attraction and you can find many fun activities on the Island. You can rent cabanas, enjoy the waterpark, flamingo watch, and go banana boat riding.
